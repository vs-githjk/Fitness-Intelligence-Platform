# FitIntel 360 v0.5.0 — Workout Intelligence

Release date: 2026-07-17
Previous release: [v0.4.2](../roadmap.md)
Alembic head: `20260716_0012`

## Summary

v0.5.0 completes the **Workout Intelligence** milestone. It builds on the prior
programming, assignment, and execution work with deterministic, read-only
training-load and adherence analytics, recorded bests over a trainee's available
completed history, coach session review, explicit whole-workout skipping, and
read-only demo analytics. This release adds no AI, wearable, messaging,
notification, automated-progression, or medical-decision capability. Health Index
and Daily Intelligence scoring are unchanged.

All analytics in this release are derived, deterministic, and read-only. They
never mutate execution history and never make a medical judgement.

## Major trainee capabilities

- Scheduled workout calendar and workout detail with explicit status.
- **Explicit whole-workout skipping** before a session starts, with an
  *everyday* (ordinary) or *wellbeing* (safety-related) kind, a bounded reason,
  and an optional note. A skip creates no workout session.
- Resumable workout execution across all five exercise tracking modes, set
  logging, per-exercise skipping, session completion, and incomplete-session
  handling (unchanged from prior phases, verified for release).
- In-execution readiness context and workout safety reporting (unchanged from
  prior phases, verified for release).
- A read-only Workouts page with:
  - **Weekly training load** (planned vs. completed) in Monday–Sunday buckets.
  - **Adherence** classifications across the reporting window.
  - **Recorded bests** across the trainee's available completed compatible
    history.
  - Workout history and per-session load summaries.

## Major coach capabilities

- Read-only **workout session review** for assigned trainees.
- Training-load and adherence analytics per trainee.
- Recorded bests per trainee (all-available-history semantics).
- **Explicit skip visibility** — ordinary and wellbeing skips are listed with a
  neutral, non-diagnostic classification and the persisted reason. A wellbeing
  skip is *not* a workout safety report and is presented separately.
- Safety-report inbox, acknowledgement, and resolution remain above analytics in
  the coach workspace (unchanged from prior phases).

## Workout Intelligence scope

The analytics engine lives in an isolated, pure package (`backend/app/analytics/`)
with no database or framework dependencies and no coupling to daily scoring. Its
calculation version is `workout-load-v1`.

### Readiness context

Readiness context captured at execution time is displayed as informational
context only. **Readiness does not provide medical clearance** and does not gate,
authorize, or prohibit exercise.

### Safety reporting

Workout safety reports are trainee-submitted records surfaced to the assigned
coach for review. **Safety reports are not continuously monitored** and do not
trigger urgent or emergency response. If a trainee feels unwell or has pain or
unusual symptoms, they should avoid exercising and seek appropriate professional
guidance.

### Load calculations

- `planned_session_load = planned_duration_minutes × target_session_rpe`
  (scheduled-workout override, else workout-template-version default; otherwise
  reported as unavailable, never `0`).
- `completed_session_load = actual_duration_minutes × session_rpe`, computed for
  terminal sessions only (completed, ended-incomplete, safety-ended).
- `session_volume_kg = Σ (actual_repetitions × actual_load_canonical_kg)` over
  completed repetitions-and-load sets only.
- Weekly load buckets are Monday–Sunday; missing data is reported as an explicit
  unavailable count, never as `0`.

**Training load is not a medical measure.** It is a deterministic bookkeeping
figure derived from prescribed and recorded workout data.

### Adherence definitions

Classification is derived at read time and never mutates history.

- `skipped` comes **only** from an explicit persisted pre-start skip
  (`ordinary_skipped` or `safety_skipped` by kind).
- Sessions that end incomplete or are safety-ended are always classified
  `partial`, even when no sets were logged. Absence of logged sets never
  downgrades a started session to skipped.
- `missed` means no session and no skip past a two-local-day grace window.
- The eligible denominator excludes coach-cancelled, superseded, and optional
  workouts. Safety skips remain in the denominator but are reported separately.

### Recorded-best semantics

A **recorded best** is the best recorded compatible completed performance in the
trainee's **available completed history** — highest recorded load, repetitions,
or volume for an exercise across its stable lineage. It is not a lifetime,
all-time, or personal-record claim, and it is scoped to available recorded data
only. Responses report `scope: "all_available_history"`.

### Explicit workout skipping

- `POST /api/v1/trainee/workouts/{scheduled_workout_id}/skip` (trainee-only).
- `skip_kind` is `ordinary` or `safety`; the reason is validated against a
  bounded set per kind; an optional note is length-limited.
- The endpoint is idempotent for a repeated identical skip and returns `409` for
  a non-eligible workout (already started, terminal, or a conflicting skip).
- A skip creates **no** workout session, and a wellbeing/safety skip creates
  **no** workout safety report.
- The endpoint enforces demo read-only protection (demo trainees receive `403`)
  and is registered in the demo mutation inventory. Coaches cannot perform this
  trainee mutation.

## Demo updates

- The deterministic demo seed now includes read-only Workout Intelligence
  examples: completed resistance workouts (kilogram and pound inputs), and a demo
  trainee with backdated, in-window explicit skips (ordinary and wellbeing),
  a partial-with-work session, a partial session with zero logged sets, a missed
  workout, and a cancelled workout, so coach analytics and skip visibility are
  demonstrable read-only.
- Demo workspaces remain read-only: all workout mutations, including skipping,
  return `403`.

## Migration information

- New migration `20260716_0012_workout_skip` adds four nullable columns
  (`skip_kind`, `skip_reason`, `skip_note`, `skipped_at`) to `scheduled_workout`
  and extends the scheduled-workout status enum with `skipped`.
- v0.5.0 also includes `20260716_0011_workout_load_summary` (immutable
  one-row-per-terminal-session-per-version load summary), introduced earlier in
  the milestone.
- Migrations are additive, append-only, idempotent, and dual-path (SQLite
  `batch_alter_table` and PostgreSQL). No historical migration was modified. No
  destructive migration is present.
- Alembic head after upgrade is `20260716_0012`.

## Upgrade notes

- Deploy is additive: run `alembic upgrade head` (the Render pre-deploy command)
  to reach `20260716_0012`; no backfill or data migration is required.
- No environment-variable changes are required for this release.
- Demo data is seeded only through the explicit seed command; production/staging
  do not seed on startup.

## Known limitations

- Readiness context is informational and provides no medical clearance.
- Safety reports are reviewed asynchronously by the coach and are not
  continuously or urgently monitored.
- Training load and analytics are deterministic bookkeeping, not medical
  measures.
- There is no automated progression or deloading; all programming remains
  coach-authored.
- Recorded bests reflect only available recorded compatible completed
  performance, not lifetime or all-time records.
- Post-completion correction of a finished session remains deferred.
- Platform limitations from prior releases remain (no rate limiting, email
  verification, MFA, audit trail, or invitation email delivery); this repository
  targets synthetic staging evaluation and is not cleared for real health data.

## Deferred features

Deferred until explicitly approved (unchanged from the roadmap):

- Native mobile applications or PWA packaging.
- Automated clinical decisions, medical diagnosis, injury prediction, treatment
  recommendations, or medical clearance.
- Autonomous workout or nutrition adjustment from readiness.
- Wearables, messaging, notifications, media uploads, and payments.
- Post-completion execution corrections.
- Marketplace, billing, insurance, employer, or clinical-system integrations.
- Any use of real health data before security, privacy, and legal gates.
